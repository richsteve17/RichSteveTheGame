# Rich $teve: A Master Record of the Career of Steve Coleman
### Independent Pro Wrestling — 2006 to 2019
*Compiled April 13, 2026. All details sourced directly from Steve Coleman's documented history.*

---

## Who He Is

**Steve Coleman.** Ring name: **Rich $teve.** 6'1", 340 lbs. From **Philadelphia, PA.**

Thirteen years on the independent wrestling circuit, 2006 to 2019. A heel — specifically a *Chicken Shit Mastermind* — who worked as a manager before he worked as a wrestler, not because managing was his identity, but because age restrictions barred him from competing when he started at sixteen. The suit was always temporary. The ring was always the destination.

His style was **Cerebral.** His move set was economical — Shoulder Block, Headlock, Body Slam, Back Elbow, Nerve Hold — with a signature called the **Market Crash** (a Side Effect) and a finisher called the **Money Drop** (a Big Splash). He was not a high-flyer. He was not a submission specialist. His real weapon was always the microphone and the long game.

His ratings, as he earned them over thirteen years: Power 71, Speed 65, Technical 79, Toughness 78, **Mic 97, Heat 96, Overall 91.** The mic number and the heat number are what matter. Everything else was in service of those two.

The core philosophy of his career — what he called the **Undeniable Code** — was built on three things: humility, study, and role-knowledge. Know what your role is. Study the business. Check your ego at the door so that when you get a moment, it lands. He also believed, deeply and practically, in what he called the **Credibility Rule**: always build your opponent up, because beating a nobody means nothing, and beating a somebody means everything. You cannot cut corners on the build.

The **Suit Rule** was simple: he wore a suit unless he was actively wrestling. He was a manager when he was suited. He was a competitor when the suit came off. The suit was not a costume — it was a statement about which mode he was operating in.

## Era 1: The Beginning — ACPW, Philadelphia (2006–2007)

### August 12, 2006 — Managing Debut
**Northeast Community Center, Philadelphia, PA.**

Steve Coleman is sixteen years old. Age restrictions bar him from competing. So the promotion puts him at ringside instead, managing **Living Dead 2k6**: the tag team of **KJ Hellfire** (flame mask, aggressive style) and **Korpse** (the man who would become his Road Dog for twelve years).

He comes out in black eye makeup and a suit. He has a microphone he doesn't need to be handed. The team he is managing loses to Dragonfly and Sebastian Rose. It does not matter. The kid who will become Rich $teve is already working the crowd before anyone in that building knows his name.

The trainers at the ACPW Academy were **Onslaught** and **Gemini.** Onslaught in particular — a power wrestler — was the man who first taught Steve the business.

### April 7, 2007 — In-Ring Debut
Steve Coleman steps into the ring for the first time as a competitor. His opponent: **Onslaught** — his own trainer. The man who taught him stands across from him for his debut match.

### March 2007 — Managing Solo
After the Living Dead 2k6 period, Steve managed Korpse as a solo act through March 2007.

### August 18, 2007 — The Grand Championship Shot That Wasn't
Steve was booked to win the **ACPW Grand Championship.** His opponent: **Ruben Ortiz** — untrained, unsafe, a danger in the ring. The match was called a **Double DQ**, both men disqualified. Steve never got the pin. The title did not change hands. He was booked to win, and the opportunity was taken from him by someone who should not have been in that position.

### August 25, 2007 — The Spike
Three-way hardcore match. **Southern Enforcer** uses a spike — his signature weapon — and it connects on Steve. The injury sends Steve Coleman into a **years-long break from the business.**

### October 6, 2012 — The Last Match Before the Gap
**ACPW/3FX Entertainment.** Steve's opponent: **Don E. Allen** — an ECW original, described as "49 F'N' Don E. Allen," black leather pants, red boots, yellow elbow pads, ACPW Internet Television Champion at the time. This is the last documented ACPW match before the long absence. After this: years away.

## Era 2: The Return — OTCW, Berkeley Springs, WV (2016)

The gap from 2007 to 2016 is not fully documented here. What is documented is where Steve Coleman resurfaces: **OTCW** — a promotion based out of the **Triple B Arena** in **Berkeley Springs, West Virginia.** He arrives not as a competitor but as an operator.

### July 30, 2016 — The OTCW Declaration
**OTCW Uncivil War.** Steve is in the building. **Big Mike Scott** — the ring announcer, the beloved voice of the locker room — is tasked with interviewing Steve post-match. Steve grabs the microphone out of his hand.

What comes out of his mouth, verbatim:

> *"My name is Rich $teve. And with Daddy's Money and attorney Donnie Diablo — I am declaring myself the General Manager of OTCW."*

OTCW management argues. The crowd is confused. Big Mike stares at him. Steve's follow-up: *"My attorneys have already filed the paperwork. This is not a negotiation."*

He is aligned — at this stage — with **Crazii Shaii** (Erik Shea), and the OTCW infrastructure around **Sheriff Richard J. White.** Big Mike was aligned with this same group. This is the entry point.

The faction that begins forming around Steve in OTCW is called **The Coalition.** Original members include: **Drew Bronson** (lightning bolt chest tattoo, power wrestler), **Allistar Conrad** (British gimmick, Union Jack flag waved by Steve at ringside), **Jesse Skelton** (cowboy hat, denim vest, heavily tattooed), **Tony Link,** and others. Steve manages them all in a suit. He is the GM. He is not yet competing.

### August 27, 2016 — Proving Ground
**OTCW Proving Ground, Triple B Arena, Berkeley Springs, WV.** Steve still in manager mode. On this night he manages three victories: Jesse Skelton wins, Tony Link wins, Justin Tyme wins. The Coalition is taking shape around him, and the in-ring return is approaching. He is not yet competing himself.

### September 24, 2016 — September to Remember / The Suit Comes Off
**OTCW September to Remember, Triple B Arena, Berkeley Springs, WV.**

This is the night the suit comes off. Steve walks to the ring, unbuttons the shirt, and underneath is the orange singlet. **In-ring competition officially begins** for Rich $teve — nearly a decade after his last real ACPW match.

The Coalition formally assembles on this night. Ten members: Sly Scarpone, Jesse Skelton, Little Monster Binky, Ray Rumble, Johnny Xross, Allistar Conrad, Drew Bronson, Tony Link, Justin Tyme, and Rich $teve.

The night comes with a cost. Tony Link faces Justice in a match with a **shaving stipulation.** Link loses. As Link's manager, Steve takes the consequence: **his head gets shaved.** Then Steve and Justin Tyme face **Buddah** (OTCW heavyweight, one of the top names in Berkeley Springs) — and they lose.

## The Coalition Arrives at Rampage Pro Wrestling (2016–2017)

Rampage Pro Wrestling was based at the **Delaware Agricultural Museum** in **Dover, Delaware.** It was the dominant promotion in the region — a real locker room, a real fanbase, a real culture. The dominant faction when Steve arrives is **Riot City's Most Wanted (RCMW):** **Siccend** (red mohawk/bandana, studded vest, the leader), **Vic Ramone** (technical wrestler), and **Matt Wylde** (who will become someone else entirely). Managed by **Kory Cross.**

Context on Kory Cross before all of this: he was one-third of a unit called **Working Class Heroes** alongside **Ryan Vox** and **Yams the Working Man.** At Rampage, Vox tagged in a team called **Motley Tü** with Ian Cross (Kory's brother) — until Kory left Motley Tü to manage RCMW instead.

### December 9, 2016 — Winter War (Rampage Debut)
**Rampage Pro Wrestling Winter War, Delaware Agricultural Museum, Dover, DE.**

The Coalition's first Rampage appearance: Johnny Xross, Drew Bronson, and Allistar Conrad, managed by Rich $teve, against Riot City's Most Wanted (Matt Wylde, Vic Ramone, Siccend) managed by Kory Cross. **The Coalition loses.** First visit. Enemy territory. They make the statement anyway, and they leave with heads up.

This is also Steve Coleman's documented **Rampage Pro Wrestling debut date: December 9, 2016.**

### March 11, 2017 — Spring Brawl (The Tide Turns)
**Rampage Pro Wrestling Spring Brawl, Delaware Agricultural Museum, Dover, DE.**

Rematch. Six-man tag, elimination rules. Johnny Xross, Ray Rumble, and Allistar Conrad (with Rich $teve) defeat Matt Wylde, Vic Ramone, and Siccend (with Kory Cross). **The Coalition wins.** The Coalition has established itself in Rampage territory.

### Capital Chaos — April 8, 2017
Documented show. **Little Monster Binky** (4'11", 120 lbs, Queens, NY) participates in the first-ever women's match in Rampage Pro Wrestling history on this date. She is part of the Talent Agency.

## The Formation of Project Mayhem (2017)

### The Riot City Rules Match
Following the Spring Brawl win, the Coalition and RCMW face each other in a **Riot City Rules** match — the payoff of the entire feud. Mid-match, Matt Wylde stops. He looks at Riot City's Most Wanted. He looks at Rich $teve. He crosses the ring.

He renames himself **Mac Mayhem.** Kory Cross follows him.

This is the birth of **Project Mayhem.**

Mac Mayhem's real name is **Matt Wylde.** He betrayed his partners mid-match to join Steve, and he brought Kory Cross with him. The exploder suplex through a table that he put Vic Ramone through in the process: the physical punctuation on the turn.

Steve then brought **Ryan Vox** (The Livewire, 5'7", 170 lbs, Philadelphia) into the group. The logic was direct: Vox had a pre-existing relationship with Yams the Working Man — they had been in Working Class Heroes together, and Vox knew where Yams lived, knew his dogs, could calm them down. Vox was the key to a later plan. **Wrex Savage** — a face who had been working the circuit and felt overlooked, specifically by Jay Cortez — crossed the ring and joined as well. Wrex described later that Project Mayhem operated like a cult: *"We are your wasted life."* The Fight Club logic of it drew people who felt cast aside.

The full Project Mayhem core lineup:
- **Rich $teve** — the Mastermind, the lead
- **Mac Mayhem** (Matt Wylde) — power, the muscle
- **Ryan Vox** (The Livewire) — high-flyer, wildcard
- **Wrex Savage** — brawler
- **Kory Cross** — manager (not a wrestler)
- **#BRUH** (Johnny Xross and Ray Rumble) — tag team, carried over from the Coalition

#BRUH were the original Coalition members Steve brought from OTCW. Both from Washington, DC. He kept them through every Coalition rebuild, through every personnel purge — when he dumped Conrad and Bronson, he kept #BRUH. When he streamlined the roster heading into Rampage, #BRUH stayed. They were, in Steve's own words, his day ones. Their manager in Rampage was **George Burkett.**

## Jay Cortez and the Feud That Lit Everything

### June 24, 2017 — The Interference
Rich $teve is beating the snot out of **Jay Cortez** when **Big Mike** runs out with a baseball bat and interferes.

Steve posted the next day:

> *"I want to know who in the hell let Big Michael Scott into Rampage Pro Wrestling last night. You retired. Everyone cried. They chanted for you... You just HAD to get involved when I was finally giving Jay Cortez what he had coming to him. If there's a new sheriff in town, then I guess I'm gonna be fighting the law."*

Big Mike took the GM job at Rampage after this moment. The entire 2017 feud with Big Mike was ignited by this one interference.

**Jay Cortez** was the **Higher Power of Project Mayhem** — revealed later. Not a trained wrestler. His son **Mike Sweeney** was beaten down by Steve at some point, deepening the personal stakes. Jay waited in the background through everything, then revealed himself as the 4th member of Project Mayhem at Big Mike's Curtain Call — cementing the final lineup.

Big Mike, for his part, had a long history. He was the ring announcer at OTCW and Rampage, beloved by the locker room and the crowd. Steve's public opinion of him, quoted in Kyngs and Qweens Magazine:

> *"My biggest nemesis would have to be that scum sucking, low life, barely literate, fat, uneducated, second rate, Dusty Rhodes wanna be Michael Scott."*

Big Mike's reply: *"Last time I checked last Saturday night I tossed your ass over the top rope."*

Steve's argument against Big Mike was philosophical: he was a ring announcer. He read what he was handed. He said *excuse me sir, what is your height, weight and where are you from* while real athletes prepared to fight. What had he actually done? The admiration the locker room had for Big Mike was, in Steve's view, completely unearned. That argument was the spine of the feud. Big Mike also authored a memoir during this period: **"The Weird One."**

## Impact Society — SSW and the Multi-Territory Run (2017)

Separate from the Rampage storyline — running *parallel* to it through all of 2017 — Steve was building his in-ring credibility in other promotions with a tag team called **Impact Society.** His partner: **Adam Armstrong,** described as his cousin. Two men from Baltimore taking their game beyond Delaware.

### April 8, 2017 — AWA Spring Fever
**Appalachian Wrestling Alliance, 2nd Annual Spring Fever. Ellamore VFD, Ellamore, WV.** Falls Count Anywhere triple threat for the AWA Tag Team Championship: Impact Society vs High Voltage vs The Rapprochement (Fleck & Slice). **Impact Society lost.**

### August 26, 2017 — SSW Summer Haze 3
**Super Star Wrestling, Summer Haze 3.** Triple Threat Stocking Stuffer Contract match: Impact Society vs Kristopher Kollof & Mal Havock vs Detroit's Finest. **Impact Society won** — earning the cash-in contract for the SSW Tag Team Championships.

### November 11, 2017 — SSW November Reign — First Championship
**Super Star Wrestling, November Reign.** Impact Society (Rich $teve and Adam Armstrong) cash in the Stocking Stuffer Contract against **The Big New Yorkers** for the **SSW Tag Team Championships.** They win. **This is Rich $teve's first championship.** November 11, 2017.

### February 10, 2018 — Code Red Wrestling, "Heartbreak City"
**Code Red Wrestling, Heartbreak City. Ellamore, WV.** Impact Society challenges The Rapprochement (Fleck & Slice) for the Code Red Tag Team Championships. **Impact Society lost.**

## The Big Mike War — Rampage 2017

Big Mike ran a retirement tour through 2017. The crowd loved it. Steve was disgusted by it. Throughout the year, Steve methodically disassembled Big Mike's life — not just his wrestling career: taking his wife's support, getting him fired from his job outside wrestling, turning his best friend against him.

The core of it was ideological. A ring announcer who had never taken a bump, who read cards he was handed, was being treated like a hero. Steve believed that was a direct insult to everyone who bled in that ring.

### November 4, 2017 — Retribution (5-on-5)
**Rampage Pro Wrestling Retribution.** The stipulation: if Steve's team wins, Big Mike's career as ring announcer ends. Big Mike managed **Team Rampage.** Steve built his team — and here is where the seed of #BRUH's betrayal was planted.

Steve's five: Mac Mayhem, Ryan Vox, Wrex Savage, **ROH star Grizzly Redwood** (hired as an outside mercenary), and **Rich $teve himself** — naming himself the 4th man at this event as the visible statement that he was now Project Mayhem's in-ring lead.

He did not put #BRUH on the card. His day ones — the men he brought from OTCW, the men he kept through every Coalition rebuild — sat home watching the 5-on-5. Steve went outside the faction entirely to hire Grizzly Redwood rather than use Johnny Xross and Ray Rumble.

**Project Mayhem wins.** **R.D. Cordova** (one half of 410 Massiv, Team Rampage) is the man Steve pins to end Big Mike's ring announcing career. The most consequential pin of the Rampage era.

### December 9, 2017 — Big Mike's Curtain Call (#BRUH Turns)
Big Mike's retirement send-off show. Named in his honor. The main event: a **25-man Randolph Scott Memorial Rumble** — named after Big Mike's own father.

Big Mike eliminates Steve from that Rumble. Steve: *"You got lucky Michael Scott, that rumble was mine."*

At this same show — five weeks after Retribution, five weeks after Steve hired an outside mercenary instead of using them — **#BRUH (Johnny Xross and Ray Rumble) turn on Project Mayhem** and align with Big Mike. George Burkett aligns with them.

Steve finds Johnny Xross near the cage after the show. What he says, alone, without a crowd:

> *"I want you to remember one thing, #Bruh. I created you. I made you both who you are. Without me you would be nothing. You made the biggest mistake you could when you crossed us."*

And separately, also documented:

> *"You betrayed what was supposed to be a real friendship. For what? For some idiot in a stupid hat."*

The idiot in a stupid hat is Big Mike. That is the cage speech. It was delivered to Xross's face because Xross was the one still standing there. But every word was about both of them — #BRUH as a collective. Ray Rumble was not there. That did not make Ray less of a target.

Also on this night: Jay Cortez reveals himself as the 4th member of Project Mayhem — the Higher Power. The final lineup is locked in.

Big Mike then exploits the contract loophole: the stipulation only banned him as ring announcer, not as GM. He returns as Rampage GM.

## January 20, 2018 — Steve vs Korpse: The Only Time
**ACPW, Neumann University, Aston, PA.**

Steve had been saying no every time ACPW called. Then they told him who the opponent was: **Korpse.** His original Road Dog. Best friends for twelve years. The man he stood beside at ringside at sixteen, the man he managed, the man he came up with.

Steve's pre-match promo:

> *"When I was first approached by ACPW and asked if I would be a part of their return show... my response was no. ACPW is my past. I was that little greenhorn who thought he knew everything. I mean, I pretty much did know everything, but since I've left there, I have continued to evolve. Why would I grace them with my presence?"*

> *"But then they told me: 'We want you to wrestle Korpse.' That got my attention. Where the hell has Korpse been? He has done absolutely nothing of note since ACPW closed. An insane asylum? Wow."*

> *"You have somebody who has not left a one-cell room for years going up against a man who is at the peak of his career — no, who is going higher every single day."*

> *"I'm not that little 16-year-old kid you posted in the video. I'm a man now. You? You were just a conversation piece. People don't remember your name. They just go, 'There was a guy who looked like a midget Undertaker and wore an ICP shirt.'"*

> *"Korpse, I am going to show you that the student has far, far surpassed the teacher. And when I pin you — because I will — you're gonna look up at me with those cold dead eyes and realize that I always have been, and I always will be, better than you. One. Two. Three."*

**Korpse wins.** The teacher defeats the student. Their first and only match as opponents. January 20, 2018.

## The Year of Mayhem — Rampage 2018

By 2018, Project Mayhem had completely restructured:
- Conrad and Bronson were long gone — purged when their value ran out
- #BRUH were now enemies
- Mac Mayhem was drifting — the **"Emphasis on ME"** confrontation between Steve and Mac over who the true leader of the faction was had already occurred, signaled by a promo from Mac that made the tension public. Mac held the **Valhalla Visions Championship** (a belt commissioned by photographer **Robbie Blizzard,** traveling and homed at RPW) during this period.
- Vox and Kory were still inside the machine but not for much longer
- **Killian McMurphy** was being positioned as the new Mac — the power replacement for Mac's drifting — and had beaten Ryan Vox for the RPW Open Challenge Championship
- **KTB (Killing The Business)** — Myles Millennium and Tim Taylor — were being worked into the orbit as the new Vox/Kory

The stated public enemy for all of 2018 was **Yams the Working Man** — the Rampage Heavyweight Champion. The real plan was running underneath.

### March 10, 2018 — Vengeance / The Riot Rumble
**Rampage Pro Wrestling "Vengeance," Delaware Agricultural Museum, Dover, DE.**

The Riot Rumble: last man standing wins a guaranteed title contract, any Rampage championship, any time, any place of his choosing.

Steve enters as **entrant number one.** He immediately slides out the *bottom* rope — not over the top, which is the only method of elimination. He cannot be eliminated. He sits at ringside. He does commentary. He watches every other man in the match wear themselves out for thirty-plus minutes. Then he gets back in.

Like Vince McMahon at the 1999 Royal Rumble. Technically legal.

He last eliminates **E.B. Cohen** ("Pancakes," a member of The Talent Agency) to win the briefcase.

Cohen immediately protests. Entrant number one slid out the bottom rope — does that count? The lockbox is **frozen, held up, pending a formal ruling.** A protest match is scheduled.

On the same night: **The Bully Club** defeated **#BRUH** (with George Burkett) to become the NEW Rampage Tag Team Champions. #BRUH would spend three months chasing those belts.

Ryan Vox wins the R.P.W. Open Challenge Championship at this same show — **March 10, 2018.**

### April 21, 2018 — Apocalypse / The Guerrero Special
**Rampage Pro Wrestling "Apocalypse," Delaware Agricultural Museum, Dover, DE.**

The protest match. If Cohen wins, the briefcase changes hands.

Steve's plan: the **Guerrero Special.** Inspired by Eddie Guerrero's legend — a tribute to one of the great all-time masters of deception.

He smiles huge at Cohen right before the bump. Full knowledge. Then he tosses the Riot Rumble lockbox to Cohen, takes the hit, and sells a **concussion so convincingly that building security calls for medical personnel.** A medical team is summoned for what appears to be a genuine emergency.

The DQ bell rings. The act drops entirely. Steve stands up cold. Security bows on the way out.

Contract retained. Cohen's protest is finished.

### June 23, 2018 — Stairway to Gold II (Title Night)
**Rampage Pro Wrestling, Stairway to Gold II.** Two championship matches define the night:

1. **Yams the Working Man** defeats **Beard Villain Johnny Malloy** for the R.P.W. Heavyweight Title via cash-in. Yams cashed in a contract on Malloy. Johnny Malloy was **never pinned or submitted in Dover.** Malloy never got his immediate rematch because Rich $teve inserted himself into the picture with the Riot Rumble lockbox — hovering at ringside during all of Malloy's summer 2018 matches and teasing the cash-in constantly. (One of those ringside appearances got Steve beaten down by **Bull James** — Bull Dempsey, with NXT/WWE history — who pulled him by the hair into the ring apron.) Malloy watched Steve leapfrog his own rematch entirely and, rather than being furious, respected it: *"It takes a special type of sicko to do that. A real gaul."*

2. **#BRUH (Johnny Xross and Ray Rumble),** managed by George Burkett, defeat **The Bully Club** in a **Ladder Match** to win the **Rampage Tag Team Championships.** This is the same Bully Club that took the belts from them at Vengeance (March 10). #BRUH reclaim them. This is peak Rampage — both titles changing hands in a single night.

### The Bathtub Promo / Home Invasion
At some point in 2018 before July, Ryan Vox — who had been in Working Class Heroes with Yams, who knew where Yams lived, who knew the dogs and could calm them down — joined Steve for an **invasion of Yams' home.** Vox was the key. He made it possible.

Vox did not expect what he found when they got in: **Steve sitting in Yams' basement, wiping himself with a picture of Working Class Heroes** — Yams, Vox, and Kory together. Vox said it was cool. The seed was planted.

**The bathtub promo** was filmed from Yams' own bathroom. Steve in Yams' tub. The psychological warfare that followed spread across social media. The **"Hijacked"** gimmick — where Steve would take opponents' social media passwords and go live on their accounts, invading their digital space the same way he invaded their physical space. The documented instances included invading Big Mike's book signing and going into Yams' home.

### July 14, 2018 — The Book Signing / Big Mike Day
**The Book Warehouses, Lancaster, PA.**

Big Mike is doing a signing for **"The Weird One."** Steve shows up. The promo, verbatim:

> *"We found Big Mike Day! Look, I can't forget my contract — you never know, there might be a Working Man around. I always got the thing on me. Cash in any time, any place, of my choosing."*

> *"Are people actually paying money to meet this guy? Meet the author, Big Mike Scott. Mike, can you sign this for me? Do you know what I got right here? The Rampage Riot Rumble contract. The one that I put you into. The retirement that Project Mayhem caused."*

> *"How many people have actually come to see this fat piece of crap today? How many? Zero. That is hilarious, Mike."*

> *"I'm gonna take these. I'll leave you two. But Mike — since you've left Rampage, things have been great. Project Mayhem has only been getting better. And one more thing: soon, I'm gonna be the Rampage Pro Wrestling Heavyweight Champion. So keep selling The Weird One. I'm taking my water back. Have a great day, folks."*

He takes the candy. He takes Big Mike's water. He leaves. Zero customers had come to the signing.

### July 22, 2018 — The Rap Battle / Steve Gets Bloodied
At an **Outbreak Pro Wrestling** post-show concert, Yams the Working Man interrupts Steve's rap group's set with a rap battle challenge. It ends in a **brawl. Steve is bloodied.** He posts the photos. He writes that on August 18th he will have retribution at Infinity CosPlayers Unite.

### August 18, 2018 — Infinity CosPlayers Unite / The Beard Shave
Steve appears earlier in the show as **"Scams the Working Man"** — yellow shirt, long wig, fake beard, a MINI traffic cone (Yams carries a large one he named **Quinton**), and a Rampage Heavyweight Title made from a **toilet paper box** ("because the title means shit to me").

The main event: Yams successfully defends the Heavyweight Title against Vic Ramone. Exhausted. The referee is called. The lockbox is opened. Inside: **a pair of shears. Not a cash-in contract.**

Steve, with KTB (Myles Millennium and Tim Taylor) and Jay Cortez, uses the shears to **shave Yams' beard** in the ring — humiliation, not title victory. The crowd assumed this was the cash-in. It was not.

Vox and Kory attack Steve to stop it. **They turn face. Working Class Heroes reforms** — Yams, Vox, and Kory. The faction reunites.

Steve walks out backstage and opens a door. **Beard Villain Johnny Malloy** is standing there — the backstage encounter captured in a Rampage video still. Malloy's line: *"Whoever wins, I'm waiting on the other side."* Steve on Malloy: *"I still respect him because he did bring people in for Dover vs. The World."*

KTB had already been beaten by #BRUH earlier in the same night in a 4-Way Tag match. They were available and angry. Steve had been working to convert them. Cosplayers Unite was the confirmation. KTB filled the exact vacancy Vox and Kory just created by turning face.

## October 13, 2018 — The Lethal Lottery / The Solo Tag Title Heist

**PTV Lethal Lottery, Rampage Pro Wrestling.**

The Lethal Lottery: random pairings for a tag tournament. The random draw pairs **Rich $teve** with **Ray Rumble** — his former tag team member, sworn enemy, the man whose manager had publicly predicted the betrayal before it happened.

George Burkett is the **Cassandra of this story.** On October 2, 2018 — when the pairing is announced — Burkett publicly states that Rich $teve cannot be trusted with Ray Rumble as his partner. He says it to Ray's face. He posts about it. He warns anyone who will listen. He identifies all three layers of the plan:

1. Malloy was never a real target — just psychological sport.
2. Yams gave Steve a free title shot without Steve needing the contract, because Steve had broken him completely.
3. The briefcase — the Riot Rumble lockbox — was **always for #BRUH.**

The crowd called Burkett paranoid. Nobody listened.

Steve runs a **calculated three-stage social media campaign** to win Ray Rumble's trust. He is methodical. He is patient. He documents every step.

**Johnny Xross is not at this show.** He is home. That is the structure of the plan — not that Xross is less of a target, but that Ray is the one in the ring that night.

They win the tournament together. In the moment before the Money Drop — the finisher, the Big Splash — Steve finds George Burkett's eyes across the ring. He **smiles.** Burkett's face drops.

Ray Rumble goes up.

Steve pins their opponents. Steve and Ray Rumble are the new **Rampage Tag Team Champions** — with Steve holding both title belts as the **only** man whose name is on them. A solo tag championship heist. #BRUH will never get those titles back.

Steve's post-match gloat, the next day, thanking Burkett by name:

> *"Ray Rumble should have listened to George Burkett. Now #BRUH will never get their titles back. Picture and format stolen — like the championship — from Mr. Burkett."*

He stole Burkett's photo format for the post. The aesthetic of the man who warned everyone about him — stolen along with the belts.

## April 27, 2019 — The Final Shot

**Rampage Pro Wrestling, The Final Shot.** The last major Rampage event. The stipulation: Jay Cortez is pinned, Big Mike becomes GM.

**Jay Cortez gets pinned.** Big Mike Scott becomes Rampage Pro Wrestling GM — the final outcome of the entire multi-year arc. The stipulation's fulcrum is not a competitive wrestling result; it is a consequence attached to Jay's presence.

Kory Cross is on Team Big Mike at this show — managing, not wrestling. Yams the Working Man is the only actual wrestler on that side. Working Class Heroes (Yams, Vox, Kory) are united for the final conflict.

KTB and Killian McMurphy are the final generation of Project Mayhem support — the newest patsies in the same cycle Steve has run since OTCW: bring people in, use them, shed them when their value ends, keep the machine running.

**Rampage Pro Wrestling closes after April 2019.** The promotion ends. The run is over.

## What Was Always the Plan

Steve Coleman documented the endgame himself. The cycle, as he described it:

> *Coalition/OTCW (2016) — brought in Conrad, Bronson, used #BRUH, others; shed Conrad and Bronson once their value was spent, kept Mac. Added Vox, added Kory, added Wrex; Mac began to drift. Replaced Mac with Killian McMurphy. Replaced Vox and Kory with KTB after they turned at Cosplayers Unite. Wrex remained. New patsies, same machine. It is the same story told over and over, and nobody in the audience was supposed to notice until it was already done.*

The endgame goal, stated directly:

**Win the Rampage Heavyweight Title from Yams. Hold both RPW Tag Titles as a solo champion. Hold the Heavyweight. Three belts at the head of his own promotion.** That is what all of it was always building toward. He got the Tag Titles. The Heavyweight — and the promotion — did not survive long enough for the rest.

## The People Who Mattered After

After Rampage Pro Wrestling closed in April 2019, the people from that world went on to do real things:

**Johnny Xross** created the **Pan-Afrikan World Diaspora Wrestling World Championship** — a real title, a legacy piece. He held it for 1,300+ days beginning in 2020.

**Trish Adora** — who was part of the Rampage Pro Wrestling circle alongside #BRUH through the DC wrestling scene (Xross and Rumble are from DC; she was part of that world before any of what came after) — won the Pan-Afrikan World Diaspora Wrestling World Championship and held it for **over 1,300 days** beginning in 2020. The championship Xross built became the championship Trish made historic.

**Johnny Malloy** — Beard Villain, Talent Agency, the man Steve publicly respected — was **never pinned or submitted** in Dover. That stands.

**Big Mike Scott** — the ring announcer, the beloved voice, the author of "The Weird One," the man Steve spent two years systematically dismantling — became Rampage GM at The Final Shot. He outlasted Steve Coleman in Rampage Pro Wrestling.

## The Enduring Truth

Steve Coleman was never booked as a babyface. He was always the heel. But the story he lived through — the architecture of it, the patience of it, the twelve years from black eye makeup at sixteen to a solo two-belt championship heist at a Lethal Lottery — is not a villain's story. It is a craftsman's story.

He built things. He built factions and then rebuilt them when the pieces stopped serving the purpose. He built feuds that took years to pay off. He built psychological pressure campaigns that broke people before the match was even signed. He built a career from a Northeast Community Center in West Philadelphia to the main event of every independent show he walked into, on his own terms, without a TV deal or a developmental contract.

The Credibility Rule was real because he believed it. You build your opponents up because beating someone who matters is the only win that means anything. He held to that even when — especially when — he was taking candy off a table and a man's water bottle at a bookstore signing attended by zero people.

The Road Dog won the only match they ever had against each other.

The people Steve helped build went on to do extraordinary things after the promotion ended.

George Burkett was right about everything.

Nobody listened.

*End of Record.*
