MASTER ARCHIVE: THE PROFESSIONAL WRESTLING CAREER OF STEVE COLEMAN

Subject: Steve Coleman (pka "Rich $teve") Archive Status: DEFINITIVE / UNABRIDGED / TOTAL CONTEXT Scope: 2006 – 2019 (Active Career) + The "Lost" Creative Plans

I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Steve Coleman, performing under the moniker Rich $teve, was a cerebral "Heel" and in-ring competitor active on the independent wrestling circuit from 2006 to 2019. Coleman’s career trajectory was defined by a distinct evolution: beginning as a teenage manager (due to age restrictions), he transitioned into a full-time wrestler, eventually becoming a top-tier strategist and faction leader who orchestrated some of the most complex narrative arcs in the Mid-Atlantic region (ACPW, OTCW, Rampage Pro Wrestling). His career was defined by a commitment to becoming "Undeniable"—a philosophy grounded in humility, study, and mastering the "talking" aspect of the business to ensure he was irreplaceable on any card.

II. THE PROFESSIONAL PROFILE (SHOOT)

The reality of the man behind the character. This section details Coleman's industry philosophy, tenure, and role as an educator.

1. The Vitals
  • Real Name: Steve Coleman
  • Hometown: Philadelphia, PA
  • Height: 6’1”
  • Weight: 340 lbs
  • Years Active: 13 Years (2006–2019)
  • Retirement Cause: Cumulative concussion history and the collapse of the Rampage promotion.
2. The "Undeniable" Code

Coleman operated under a strict professional ethos developed over nearly two decades of experience. This code separated him from "cosplay wrestlers" and defined his longevity. "Know your role on the show. Study everything. Always stay humble. If you keep to those things, you will be Undeniable and Irreplaceable."

3. Industry Curriculum (The Promo Seminar)

Coleman served as an educator in the business, teaching specific seminars on promo structure and psychology. This is the codified curriculum. A. The Credibility Rule

  • Concept: "Build up your opponent. Don't point out the obvious flaws. Don't break them down. Avoid their giant flaws; highlight their strengths so that your victory matters."
  • The Logic: If you bury your opponent verbally and then beat them, you defeated a nobody. If you lose, you lost to a nobody. Keeping the opponent credible elevates the entire match and ensures the fans stay invested in the outcome. B. Character Ownership
  • Philosophy: "No one will create it for you. You have to care most about the history of your character."
  • Application: Before speaking, talent must answer: Who are you? Why are you here? What is your story? If you don't care about your character's history, the fans won't either. C. Structural Mechanics (The 5 Ws)
  • The Blueprint: Every promo must hit the essentials in 1-2 minutes: Who? What? Where? When? Why?
  • The Golden Detail: Always mention the Day of the Week (especially in the final week of promotion) to drive ticket sales. D. Facebook Live Tactics
  • The Method: "Focus on the topic before focusing on the comments." Establish the narrative hook before engaging with the chat. Don't let the audience dictate the pace of the segment. E. In-Ring Psychology & Crowd Interaction
  • The Rule: "Find your mark. Look at them. Interact. Make them remember you. Get that first pop to secure the heat for the angle."
  • The Action: Interaction creates a connection that physical spots cannot. It is about making the audience feel like you are speaking directly to them, forcing them to react.

III. THE CHARACTER PROFILE (KAYFABE)

The fictional universe of Rich $teve. This section details the archetype, psychology, and presentation.

1. The Persona
  • Name: Rich $teve (Note: "Rich" is an adjective; the name is $teve).
  • Archetype: The "Chicken Shit" Mastermind. A cerebral villain who uses psychology, loopholes, and numbers to dismantle opponents.
  • Tactical Note: Rarely utilized managers (except Kory Cross for logistics) because $teve functioned as his own mouthpiece and heat magnet.
2. In-Ring Psychology & Tactics

$teve’s in-ring work was defined by tactical opportunism. He did not rely on physical dominance but on the manipulation of the referee and the environment. He prioritized cutting promos before his matches to ensure the audience was emotionally invested in his defeat before the bell ever rang. The "Guerrero Special" (The Masterstroke & The Payoff): While $teve was not defined by a specific set of "cheating" spots, he was capable of pulling off high-IQ tributes to the greats of the business. One notable masterstroke involved a direct tribute to Eddie Guerrero: tossing a weapon (specifically the Riot Rumble Lockbox) to an opponent and faking a concussion to draw a disqualification.

  • The Work: During the match, $teve sold the "concussion" so convincingly that the building—including the house security—believed it was a legitimate medical emergency.
  • The Reveal: After the bell rang and the DQ victory was secured, $teve dropped the act entirely. The "reveal" of the deception was a cold, calculated shift that showcased the character's manipulative genius.
  • The Impact: Realizing they had been genuinely "worked" by a master of the craft, the venue security guards were bowing to $teve as he exited the ringside area, recognizing the absolute brilliance of the work.
3. The Presentation
  • Finisher: The Money Drop (Big Splash).
  • Signature: Market Crash (Side Effect).
  • The Suit Rule: $teve strictly adhered to a rule of only wearing wrestling gear during active matches. For promos, segments, or managing out in the crowd, he wore high-end suits.
  • Quote: "Why would I wear gear if I'm not wrestling? That’s what idiots and poor people do."
4. Auditory Semiotics (Music Analysis)
  • Theme Song: "Jet Black New Year" by Thursday.
  • Provenance: Coleman received personal permission from vocalist Geoff Rickly to use the track during a meeting at Warped Tour when Coleman was 16.
  • Entrance Psychology: Establishing a character defined by cyclical frustration and "anti-system" defiance.
  • Exit Theme ("The Countdown"): For major victories (e.g., the Solo Tag Title win), the track was cued specifically to the bridge ("Ten seconds left until midnight...").

IV. THE CHRONOLOGICAL STORYLINE

Era 1: Origins & Foundations (2006–2012)
  • 2006: The Debut: Coleman enters the industry at age 16 as a student at the ACPW Academy under Onslaught and Gemini's tutelage. Crazii Shea is present at his very first show.
  • 2006–March 2007: The Managerial Era: Due to age restrictions, Coleman begins as a manager. He managed Living Dead 2k6 (Korpse & KJ Hellfire) for exactly one show. Following that, he served exclusively as the manager for Korpse until March 2007, one month prior to his in-ring debut. He and Korpse became best friends ("Road Dogs").
  • April 7, 2007: In-Ring Debut: Cardinal Krol Center, Springfield, PA. (Onslaught, Patch & Wrecker defeat D-Craze, Rich Steve & Vinny Hoffa).
  • Aug 18, 2007: The Grand Championship Incident: ACPW at VFW Post 1451 in South River, NJ. Coleman is booked to win the ACPW Grand Championship. The match ends in a forced Double DQ finish because opponent Ruben Ortiz was untrained and unsafe. Management killed the match to prevent Coleman from being injured.
  • 2009: The Music Era (CMASS / Black Sheep): During a wrestling hiatus, Coleman and partner Ryan DiPaulo (The Brain Cell) form CMASS (originally known as Black Sheep).
  • The Rodney King Milestone: CMASS writes the entrance song for THE Rodney King and performs it live at a celebrity boxing show for his entrance. This is the duo’s first live concert performance. Crazii Shea is present for this event.
  • "Quicksand": CMASS produces the track "Quicksand," based on a poem written by Crazii Shea and given to Coleman.
  • 2012: The Shift: Coleman returns to active wrestling at ACPW, retires the "Future of Wrestling" gimmick, and aligns with Mac Mayhem under the mantra "I AM Wrestling."
Era 2: The OTCW Resurrection & The Original Coalition
  • The Return: Crazii Shea begs Coleman to return for OTCW. Shea knew Coleman was the veteran "secret weapon" needed to dominate a new market where no one knew him.
  • The Hostile Takeover (July 30, 2016): At OTCW's "Uncivil War," $teve declares himself General Manager, citing "Daddy's Money" and legal backing from attorney Donnie Diablo.
  • The Original Coalition: $teve built his power base in OTCW by forming the first iteration of The Coalition.
  • Members: Rich $teve, Johnny Xross, Ray Rumble (#BRUH), Allistar Conrad, Drew Bronson, Jesse Skelton, and Little Monster Binky.
  • The Scalping: Coleman is forced by Shea to hand out promotional flyers in Berkeley Springs twice a month. The rivalry culminates in a match where Coleman is handcuffed to Sheriff Jay White. Upon the Coalition losing, Crazii Shea "scalped" Coleman with a knife while White and Big Mike held him down.
Era 3: The Rampage Invasion & Project Mayhem
  • The Invasion: $teve narrows the group for a surgical strike on Rampage Wrestling. The invading Coalition crew consisted of: Rich $teve, Johnny Xross, Ray Rumble, Drew Bronson, and Allistar Conrad. They feud with Riot City's Most Wanted (RCMW) (Siccend, Vic Ramon, Matt Wylde).
  • The Turn: During the payoff of the Coalition vs. RCMW Riot City Rules match, Matt Wylde betrays Riot City to join Steve. He renames himself Mac Mayhem.
  • Project Mayhem Phase 1: Merged Rich $teve, Mac Mayhem, Kory Cross (Manager), and #BRUH. Drew Bronson and Allistar Conrad are kicked "to the curb" to streamline the group.
  • Project Mayhem Phase 2: The Livewire Ryan Vox and Wrex Savage join the faction.
  • The Big Mike Saga: Ring announcer Big Mike tries to get tough during $teve's beatdown of Jay Cortez's 16-year-old son, Mike Sweeney. A 5v5 match is set: if Team Rampage loses, Mike retires. $teve secures the pin on RD Cordova (of 410 Massiv) to end Mike's career as an announcer.
  • The GM Loophole: After a year away, Big Mike returns as the new General Manager. $teve sells the realization of the loophole (the contract only forbid him from being an announcer) purely through visual expression (no words all visual).
  • The Solo Tag Title Heist: * Leaving Mayhem (December 2017): #BRUH turns Face and leaves Project Mayhem. Rich $teve vows revenge before leading a beatdown of the duo by Project Mayhem.
  • Rise of BRUH: Now fan favorites, #BRUH go on to capture their first title, the Rampage Pro Wrestling Tag Team Championships, led by manager George Burkett.
  • Lethal Lottery (October 2018): In a "Lethal Lottery," $teve and Ray Rumble are assigned as partners which left Johnny Xross not present at the show. After losing to Riot City’s Most Wanted in the semifinals of the tournament, sensing the vulnerable nature that Ray was in, $teve hit the Money Drop finisher on his former partner, cashed in his riot rumble contract, and pinned him to hold both Tag Team Championship belts as a solo competitor.
  • The Collapse?: Ego clashes lead to Mac Mayhem reclaiming his status in Project Mayhem ("Emphasis on ME"). Jay Cortez is revealed as the 4th member of team Mayhem for the scheduled Mayhem Rules Match at Big Mike’s Curtain Call (Full Circle return to the Devil's Playpen).
Era 4: The Homecoming (2018)
  • Jan 20, 2018: "No Turning Back": After years away from ACPW, $teve returns for a Grudge Match against Korpse in Aston, PA (Neumann University).
  • The Excursion Narrative: $teve frames the match as an "excursion return"—the homecoming of a Superstar who has evolved past his original "Teacher" (Korpse). This loss to Korpse was their first ever meeting as opponents in the ring.

V. MODULE: THE "HIJACKED" GIMMICK

  • Concept: Psychological warfare via social media identity theft.
  • Methodology: $teve obtains the opponent's password. He goes "Live" on their Facebook, keeping the camera on a scenic spot for 45-60 seconds to bait their friends and family into joining. Once the count spikes, he flips the camera: "Hi ladies and gentlemen, my name is Rich $teve, welcome to Hijacked."
  • The Bookstore Incident (Lancaster, PA): $teve invades Big Mike's signing for his memoir "The Weird One."
  • The Action: $teve asks the clerk for sales numbers (Zero). In front of Mike, $teve mocks the book, pulls out the Riot Rumble contract, eats the free candy on the table, drinks Mike's water, rubs the book on his balls, and leaves with the rest of the candy for the road.
  • The Yams Home Invasion: $teve and Ryan Vox break into Yams' house while he is out filming his web series "The Workday Chronicles."
  • The Action: $teve mocks Yams' Bruce Springsteen and Nickelback CDs, replacing one with a CMASS CD. He finds a framed photo of The Working Class Heroes (Yams & Vox) and wipes his backside with it while forcing Vox to film the disrespect of his own former team.
  • The Cosplay Scheme: Since the next show was "Infinity Cosplayers Unite" (Aug 18th), $teve decided his "costume" would be Yams. He stole Yams' gear (shirt/gloves) to enter as “Scams” while teasing cashing in his contract so that "Yams takes the title from Yams."
