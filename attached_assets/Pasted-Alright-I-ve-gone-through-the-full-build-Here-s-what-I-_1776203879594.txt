Alright, I’ve gone through the full build. Here’s what I’m seeing.
What’s already solid: Expo/React Native with expo-router, four-tab layout (Home, Career, Match, Roster), turn-based combat engine with signature moves, heel tactics system (Distract, Ref Manip, Guerrero), GameContext with AsyncStorage persistence, cutscene system with branching win/lose outros, promo game with crowd reactions — all functional and clean.

Some of those would immediately fill gaps. Big Mike alone would cover a major character who shows up across multiple eras.
The match engine problem is structural. match.tsx takes a single opponentId and runs a 1v1 loop. Every move hits one person, stamina tracks two fighters, win condition is one person hitting zero. To do tag matches you need: a matchType param, arrays for teammates/opponents, a legal-man system, hot tag mechanics, and the AI needs to handle partner behavior. That’s not a patch — it’s a second match engine mode that shares the UI shell.
Stipulations are the same story. The stipulation string exists on every chapter but the match engine doesn’t read it. Right now “Riot City Rules” and “6-Man Tag Elimination” and “Singles Match” all play identically. Each stipulation type needs its own win-condition logic and action set. Hardcore needs a weapons inventory. Ladder needs a climb meter. Rumble needs over-the-top elimination tracking. The Guerrero Special DQ tactic should probably be disabled in No-DQ matches — right now it works everywhere.
The Legacy/Career split is actually the simplest change architecturally. Your GameContext already has CHAPTER_ORDER and REQUIRES_WIN_TO_ADVANCE. Legacy Mode = what you have now but stricter (must win every chapter to advance, loss resets to era start). Career Mode = new GameContext variant with a branching path, opponent selection, and a reputation/heat system that carries across shows. They share the match engine, wrestler database, and all assets. The split is at the routing level — pick your mode on the home screen, each mode uses its own save state key.
